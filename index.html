The Great Spider

By Daniel Arnould

Spider, Spider, on the wall,

Before my life would take a fall,

There it sits so peacefully

As my husband and I live so blissfully.

As Brisbane winds break through,

Our father's trumpets blew,

Bereft an adventure I could not give

He left to fight and alone I live.

Spider, why is this house so big?

I swear you've moved,

Since we last met,

I can't recall

I'm so upset.

A pain that stops and starts,

Like a telegram stuck to my heart,

Fearing the news of my sweetheart

Ever since we've been apart.

A few months pass sans return,

Time to get a job, since they've unturned,

A man's job is not for me,

But I want to help with all I can

"Volunteer!" said the newsman.

Days and days,

I spend in factories,

Making care packages,

For the cavalry,

But in each of them,

I slide in case it's you,

A reminder of my love

That you know is true.

Down and down,

The spider goes,

Without the gunfire

That you must pose.

A year passes - I still cannot see,

My only love the world gave to me,

The factories closed a while ago,

Now where should I go?

A postman came with his telegram,

Now who will bring me my children?

Is the thought I am.

Spider, Spider, familiar friend,

It seems you've reached your end,

Hurry away across the floor,

I'm no use any more,

So dead with you I choose to lay

Heaven far away...
